CSS Combinators

Combinators define the relationship between two or more selectors in CSS.

Descendant Combinator (space)

This paragraph is a descendant of the div.

This nested paragraph is also a descendant of the div.

Child Combinator (>)

This paragraph is a direct child of the div.

This paragraph is not a direct child, so it won't match.

Next Sibling Combinator (+)

I am a div

This paragraph is immediately after the div and will be selected.

This paragraph is not immediately after a div, so it won't match with + combinator.

Subsequent-Sibling Combinator (~)

I am a div

This paragraph is a subsequent sibling of the div and will be selected.

Another paragraph following the div; also selected with ~ combinator.
